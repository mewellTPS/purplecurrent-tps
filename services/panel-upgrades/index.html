When a Panel Upgrade Becomes a Business Decision

A commercial electrical panel upgrade isn't a maintenance task you schedule when things are slow. It's what happens when your facility can't support the equipment you need to operate, when a failed inspection holds up a buildout, or when an aging panel puts your occupancy at risk.

Purple Current handles commercial electrical panel upgrades across Tampa Bay, from load analysis through final inspection. We size the upgrade to your actual demand, coordinate with the utility when the service entrance is involved, and pull every permit required by your jurisdiction. The work is documented, the inspections are closed, and you get records that hold up when your compliance team or insurance carrier asks for them.

We've spent 15 years doing this work for property managers, facilities directors, general contractors, and business owners. We don't do residential. Every crew we send to your site works commercial and industrial jobs every day.

Scope of Work

What's Included in a Commercial Panel Upgrade

01

Load Analysis and System Evaluation

Every panel upgrade starts with understanding what your facility actually draws and what it's going to need. We perform a full load analysis on the existing system, evaluate the condition of the current panel and service equipment, and identify whether the upgrade is a panel swap or a full service entrance replacement. You get a clear report on current capacity, projected demand, and what the upgrade needs to cover.

02

Panel and Service Entrance Upgrades

We handle the full scope of the upgrade: panel replacement, load center installation, service conductor upgrades, and meter base work when the service entrance needs to change. When a capacity increase requires utility coordination, we manage that process and keep you informed on the timeline. Branch circuit work, breaker replacement, and labeling are included as part of every panel job. The system is tested, labeled to current NEC standards, and ready for inspection when we're done.

03

Permitting, Inspection, and Documentation

Every commercial panel upgrade in Florida requires a permit. We handle the application, schedule inspections, and coordinate directly with the building department in your county. When the job closes, you receive full documentation: permit records, inspection results, and a scope summary for your files. That paper trail matters for lease compliance, insurance requirements, and future service work on the same system.

When You Need This

Common Situations That Require a Panel Upgrade

Facility Expansion or Tenant Buildout

A new tenant moving into a commercial space often brings electrical requirements the existing panel can't handle. Office electrical panel upgrades during a buildout are time-sensitive: the panel needs to be right before the rest of the electrical rough-in can proceed. We coordinate with your GC to keep the schedule on track and make sure the new capacity supports the full commercial electrical installation.

Aging Panels and Code Compliance

Older commercial buildings frequently carry panels that were sized for a different era of electrical demand. Breakers that trip under normal load, panels with no spare capacity, and equipment that predates current code are all signs the system needs to be replaced. We see this regularly in office buildings, retail centers, and multi-family properties across Tampa Bay. A panel that can't pass inspection is a panel that puts your occupancy at risk. If you're unsure whether your system needs attention, our maintenance and repair team can evaluate it.

Equipment Additions and Increased Load

New equipment means new load. Industrial electrical panel upgrades are often driven by the addition of machinery, compressors, HVAC systems, or production equipment that exceeds the panel's rated capacity. The same applies to EV charging installations, which frequently require a service upgrade before the chargers can be connected. We run the load calculations first and size the panel upgrade to handle both the new equipment and reasonable future growth.
