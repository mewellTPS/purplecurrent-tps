Overview

Whole-Building Surge Protection — Sized to Your Electrical Load

A single voltage event can take out equipment that costs more than the protection would have. Servers, HVAC controls, medical devices, production equipment — none of it is designed to absorb a surge through an unprotected service entrance.

Commercial surge protection starts at the panel and works outward. Purple Current installs surge protective devices (SPDs) at the service entrance and at branch panels throughout your facility, sized to match your load profile and the sensitivity of the equipment behind it. We handle the full scope: site assessment, SPD selection, installation, testing, and documentation. Every job is permitted and inspected.

If your building doesn't have SPDs, or the ones installed are past their service life, the next surge decides what you lose.

What We Install

Commercial Surge Protection Solutions

Purple Current provides commercial surge protection solutions across the full protection stack, from the service entrance to individual branch panels and point-of-use locations. The scope depends on your facility's load profile, equipment sensitivity, and existing electrical infrastructure.

Type 1 SPDs install at the main service entrance panel and are your first line of defense against surges entering the building from the utility side. These devices handle the highest energy levels and protect everything downstream. We size them based on your service amperage and the fault current available at your location.

Type 2 SPDs install at distribution panels and subpanels closer to the equipment they protect. For facilities with sensitive electronics, data infrastructure, or medical equipment, point-of-use protection adds a second layer that catches what gets past the service entrance device. We evaluate which panels need branch-level protection based on what's connected downstream.

In Central Florida, lightning is a primary source of surge events. Purple Current installs lightning-related surge protection as part of our SPD scope.

We offer surge monitoring and telemetry as part of our commercial surge protection services. Monitored SPDs track surge events, device status, and end-of-life indicators, giving you documentation for insurance purposes and maintenance planning. As our telemetry capabilities expand, this becomes a more detailed reporting layer across your facility's electrical systems.

When It Matters

When Surge Protection Makes Sense

Most commercial buildings don't have adequate surge protection. The ones that do often have devices that were installed during original construction and never evaluated since. Here are the situations where we see the most demand:

After a surge event damages equipment or trips breakers, property owners want protection installed before the next one hits. Insurance carriers are increasingly asking whether SPDs are in place before processing equipment damage claims.

NEC 2020 Article 230.67 requires surge protective devices on certain service entrances. If your building predates that code cycle, you may not have SPDs at all.

If you're planning a panel upgrade, adding surge protection during that project is the most cost-effective approach. The panel is already open, the electrician is already on-site, and the SPD gets installed as part of a single permitted job. The same applies to new construction and tenant buildouts.

Facilities with sensitive equipment have their own timeline. Data centers, medical offices, industrial controls, and any facility running EV charging infrastructure all carry enough risk to justify the investment before something fails.

For facilities that already have generators or UPS systems, surge protection completes the power protection stack. Generators protect against outages. UPS systems bridge the gap during transfer. SPDs protect the equipment from the surge that caused the outage in the first place.

Our Process

How We Scope and Install Commercial Surge Protection

We start with a site assessment. That means reviewing your existing electrical infrastructure, documenting your load profile, identifying the equipment and systems that need protection, and evaluating whether your current panel configuration supports SPD installation or needs work first.

From there, we select and size the SPDs to match your facility. We install at the service entrance, at branch panels where warranted, and at point-of-use locations for high-sensitivity equipment. Every installation is permitted, inspected, and documented with records you can hand to your insurance carrier or compliance team.

If surge protection is part of a larger project, whether a panel upgrade, new construction, or a retrofit, we coordinate the SPD scope into the overall job so it's handled in one mobilization, one permit, and one inspection cycle.
