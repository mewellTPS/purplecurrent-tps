What We Install

Commercial Lighting Services

Interior Commercial Lighting

Most of our lighting work is interior: offices, retail spaces, warehouses, manufacturing floors, healthcare facilities, and multi-family common areas. We install recessed troffers, high-bays, linear fixtures, downlights, and task lighting to whatever spec your architect or engineer has drawn. If the spec calls for dimming or occupancy controls, we wire and program those as part of the installation.

The work is straightforward when the planning is solid. We review the lighting plans before material ordering to flag anything that doesn't match the electrical layout or code requirements. Problems caught in plan review cost nothing. Problems caught during inspection cost everyone time.

Outdoor and Exterior Lighting

Commercial outdoor lighting installation covers building-mounted fixtures, canopy lights, walkway and perimeter lighting, and wall packs. We install photocell and timer controls so exterior lights operate on schedule without manual intervention.

Exterior work has its own code and inspection requirements. Fixture mounting heights, GFCI protection for certain locations, and weatherproofing standards all vary by county across Tampa Bay. We know the local amendments and build to them from the start.

Outdoor Lighting Photo

LED Conversion and Upgrades

If your building is still running fluorescent or HID fixtures, a full LED conversion pays for itself through lower energy costs and reduced maintenance. We replace the fixtures entirely rather than retrofitting lamps into old housings. New fixtures, new drivers, clean wiring. The result performs better and lasts longer than a lamp swap.

LED conversions in occupied spaces require planning. We phase the work to keep sections of your facility operational, coordinate shutdowns where needed, and handle disposal of old fixtures. The permitting requirements are the same as new installation — we pull the permit and schedule the inspection.

Lighting Controls and Commissioning

Purple Current installs and integrates commercial lighting controls, including dimming systems, occupancy and vacancy sensors, daylight harvesting, and scheduling systems. For clients with building automation or telemetry platforms, we tie lighting controls into the existing system so facility managers can monitor and adjust from a single interface.

After installation, we test every circuit and verify that fixtures, switches, and controls operate as specified. You get a completed system that works the day we hand it over, not a punchlist of items to chase down later.

Our Process

How a Commercial Lighting Installation Works

Every commercial lighting installation follows the same sequence, whether it's part of a ground-up build or a standalone project.

1

Plan review and coordination

We review the lighting plans and electrical drawings, confirm fixture counts and types against the spec, and identify anything that needs clarification before work starts. If we're working under a GC, we coordinate our schedule with the other trades on site.

2

Permitting

We pull the electrical permit. On most Tampa Bay commercial projects, that means submitting plans to the local building department and confirming the scope of work before we start.

3

Rough-in

We run conduit, pull wire, and install junction boxes and fixture supports according to the plans. On new construction, this happens after framing and before drywall. On retrofit projects, we work around the existing structure and route new circuits as needed.

4

Fixture installation and trim-out

Once the space is ready, we mount and connect every fixture, wire controls, and label circuits at the panel. On large jobs, this is where phasing matters most — we work section by section so you're not waiting on lighting to close out other trades.

5

Testing and inspection

We test every circuit, verify control operation, and schedule the inspection. Our goal is a clean inspection the first time. If the inspector needs to come back, that's a delay we take personally.
