Fault Diagnosis and Troubleshooting

Intermittent faults, unexplained tripping, voltage irregularities, and ground faults all have causes. Finding those causes in a commercial system takes methodical diagnosis, not guesswork. We trace the problem to its source, confirm the diagnosis, and give you a clear explanation of what's wrong before we start repair work. If the issue points to something larger, like an aging panel or undersized service, we'll tell you that upfront rather than patching over a systemic problem. Panel Upgrades & Service Upgrades

Circuit Repairs and Replacements

Failed circuits, damaged conductors, and overloaded branch wiring are common in commercial facilities that have added equipment or reconfigured spaces over the years. We repair or replace circuits to match the actual load they're carrying, not just restore them to their previous state. If the circuit was undersized to begin with, we flag it.

Panel and Breaker Repairs

Breaker failures, bus bar damage, arc flash evidence, and corrosion inside panels are all problems we see regularly in commercial facilities. We repair what can be repaired and recommend replacement when a panel has reached the point where repairs stop making economic sense. Wiring Repairs and Upgrades

Older commercial buildings frequently have wiring that predates current NEC requirements or was never designed for the loads it's now carrying. We repair damaged wiring, replace deteriorated insulation, and upgrade conductors where the existing wiring no longer meets code or capacity requirements. Maintenance Contracts

Commercial Electrical Maintenance Service Contracts

Most repair calls are preventable. The breaker that failed was showing signs of fatigue months earlier. The wiring that overheated was undersized for a load that changed two tenants ago. A commercial electrical maintenance program catches these problems before they become emergency calls.

Purple Current offers maintenance contracts for commercial and industrial facilities in Tampa Bay. Contract clients receive quarterly inspections, scheduled service visits, and priority response when unscheduled issues come up.

What a maintenance contract covers:

A quarterly inspection walks through the facility's electrical infrastructure systematically. We check panels, breakers, connections, grounding, and conductors for signs of wear, heat damage, corrosion, and code compliance issues. We document findings and flag anything that needs attention before it becomes a failure.

Between inspections, scheduled service visits cover planned work: circuit additions, equipment connections, lighting repairs, and other non-emergency tasks that are easier and cheaper to handle on a regular schedule than as one-off calls.

Contract clients also receive priority scheduling when something unexpected comes up. If you have a maintenance agreement with us and call with an urgent repair, you go to the front of the line.

The specific scope of a maintenance contract depends on your facility. Contact us with details about the building, your current electrical infrastructure, and what you need covered. We'll put together a maintenance plan that fits.
