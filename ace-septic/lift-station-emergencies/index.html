When to call ACE

Lift station pump failure or alarm sounding

The pumps have stopped, the alarm is active, or the wet well is rising. Call before it overflows.

Sewage surfacing at wet well, manhole, or parking area

Visible wastewater on the surface is a health hazard and a regulatory event. It needs immediate containment.

Multi-fixture commercial or industrial sewage backup

If more than one restroom, floor drain, or tenant space is backing up, the problem is in the system, not a single line.

Grease trap overflow threatening operations

A full or blocked grease trap can shut a kitchen down and trigger a health department visit.

Storm or flood impact on wastewater infrastructure

Saturated ground, flooded control panels, and power loss all put lift stations and collection lines at risk.

Municipal or HOA collection system backup

Neighborhood-scale backups require coordinated response with VAC trucks, bypass pumping, and regulatory communication.

Emergency services

Lift station emergency repair

Pump and motor failures don't announce themselves on a convenient schedule. ACE responds with VAC trucks to stage the wet well before diagnosis begins, buying time to isolate the problem. We handle pump replacement, motor rebuilds, float switch failures, and control panel issues in-house. ACE holds septic, plumbing, electrical, and CBC licenses, so one crew handles the full scope without waiting on subcontractors.

Lift station alarm response

An alarm means the system has detected a condition it can't resolve on its own: high water, pump failure, power loss, or a float out of position. ACE provides on-site response for alarm events and can support SCADA and telemetry-monitored stations where remote diagnostics need field confirmation. We diagnose the trigger, resolve the condition, and document what happened for your records.

[Photo: Lift station control panel]

Commercial and industrial sewage backup

Multi-fixture backups in a commercial building usually point to a force main issue, a failed pump, or a blockage downstream of the building. ACE deploys temporary bypass pumping to keep flows moving during diagnosis and repair, preventing additional damage to interiors, parking areas, and tenant operations. We work with property managers and facility teams to communicate status and timeline clearly.

Emergency grease trap pumping

A grease trap at capacity can shut down a commercial kitchen in hours. ACE provides emergency grease trap pumping for restaurants, hotels, event venues, and institutional food service operations. We pump, clean, and inspect the trap to determine whether the issue was accumulation, a blockage, or a structural failure. All service is documented for your health department records.

[Photo: Grease trap service]

Municipal and HOA wastewater emergency

Neighborhood-scale and campus-scale overflow events require more than a single truck. ACE coordinates multi-crew response for municipal and HOA collection system failures, including bypass pumping, wet well evacuation, and temporary flow management. We've supported Hillsborough County during hurricane-related wastewater emergencies and understand the documentation and communication requirements that come with public infrastructure events. Post-event reporting is provided to support Florida DEP communication.
